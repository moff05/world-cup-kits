# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences with significant overlap:

- **Kit obsessives**: People who care deeply about design history — the Adidas vs. Topper eras, fabric changes, crest evolutions, colorway decisions. They want precision and completeness.
- **Football history fans**: Casual-to-serious fans drawn to the stories — why Brazil switched from white, who wore what in the Battle of Bern, the Maracanazo's emotional weight. They want narrative and atmosphere.

The overlap is the primary target: people who see football kits as a lens on history, identity, and culture. When they visit, they're looking for both the look and the story behind it.

## Product Purpose

A cultural reference site for World Cup kit history. Every kit from every tournament, documented with annotated photographs and short essays that situate each design in its historical moment. The kit is always the entry point; the story is what makes it worth staying for.

Success looks like: a visitor opens one year and reads three more they didn't intend to.

## Brand Personality

Feels like the World Cup itself — beautiful, rich in culture, exciting. Cinematic and editorial in authority, atmospheric in tone. The site should evoke the same feeling as watching archive footage: vivid, historical, alive.

Three words: **Cinematic. Cultural. Precise.**

## Anti-references

- **FIFA.com / generic sports portals** — corporate, garish, zero editorial voice, navigation hell
- **SaaS product dashboards** — dark mode tools with metric cards and utility-first design energy; makes football feel like a spreadsheet
- **Reddit / wiki walls of text** — information-rich but visually raw; no hierarchy, no atmosphere, content dumps with no craft
- **Hypebeast / streetwear aesthetics** — Drop culture energy; high-contrast for its own sake, style over substance, wrong cultural register entirely

## Design Principles

1. **The kit is the entry point.** Every page starts with the photograph and the shirt. UI chrome should be invisible — the content creates the atmosphere, not the shell.

2. **Feel the era.** A 1978 photo should feel different from a 2022 photo. Historical quality, grain, and color temperature are features, not flaws. Preserve the atmosphere of the archive.

3. **Culture over statistics.** This site is about history, identity, and story — not scorelines and tables. Every annotation is a piece of cultural writing, not a stat card.

4. **Earned credibility, not declared authority.** The writing and photography do the work. Don't signal quality with badges, stars, or typographic hierarchy theater. Be genuinely good instead.

5. **The surface invites; the depth rewards.** A casual visitor should feel immediately oriented. An obsessive should find exactly the precision they came for. Neither should have to work for it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Key considerations:
- Annotation dots must be keyboard-accessible and have clear focus states
- Photo captions and image credits must be readable at all contrast ratios
- Reduced motion: annotation hover pulses and any future transitions need `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives
- Kit photos are decorative-adjacent but have meaningful content in captions; alt text should describe the historical context, not just "football match"
